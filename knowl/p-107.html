Notice that \(A \neq 0 \) means that at least one of its columns is not a zero vector (since at least one element). Let us assume it is the \(j \)th column, \(a_j \text{,}\) that is nonzero. Let \(e_j \) equal the column of \(I \) (the identity matrix) indexed with \(j \text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{l} \| A \|_{\mu,\nu} \\ ~~~=~~~~\lt {\rm definition} \gt \\ \max_{x \neq 0} \frac{\| A x \|_\mu}{\| x \|_{\nu}} ~~~\ge ~~~~\lt e_j {\rm ~is~a~specific~vector} \gt \\ \frac{\| A e_j \|_\mu}{\| e_j \|_{\nu}} \\ ~~~=~~~~\lt A e_j = a_j \gt \\ \frac{\| a_j \|_\mu}{\| e_j \|_{\nu}} \\ ~~~\gt~~~~\lt {\rm by~assumption~on~} j \gt \\ 0. \end{array} \end{equation*}
