Proof

The proof again build on the fact that the supremum over a compact set is attached and can be replaced by the maximum.

We will prove that there exists a \(\tau \) such that for all \(A \in \C^{m \times n} \)

\begin{equation*} \vert \vert \vert A \vert \vert \vert \leq \tau \| A \| \end{equation*}

leaving the rest of the proof to the reader.

Let \(A \in \C^{m \times n} \) be an arbitary matrix. W.l.o.g. assume that \(A \neq 0 \) (the zero matrix). Then

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{l} \vert \vert \vert A \vert \vert \vert \\ ~~~ = ~~~~ \lt {\rm algebra} \gt \\ \frac{\vert \vert \vert A \vert \vert \vert}{\| A \|} \| A \| \\ ~~~ \le ~~~~ \lt {\rm algebra} \gt \\ \left( \sup_{Z \neq 0} \frac{\vert \vert \vert Z \vert \vert \vert}{\| Z \|} \right) \| A \| \\ ~~~ = ~~~~ \lt {\rm homogenuity} \gt \\ \left( \sup_{Z \neq 0} \vert \vert \vert \frac{Z}{\| Z \|} \vert \vert \vert \right) \| A \| \\ ~~~ = ~~~~ \lt {\rm change~of~variables:~} B = Z / \| Z \| \gt \\ \left( \sup_{\| B \| = 1} \vert \vert \vert B \vert \vert \vert \right) \| A \| \\ ~~~ = ~~~~ \lt {\rm the~set~}\| B \| = 1 {\rm ~is~compact} \gt \\ \left( \max_{\| B \| = 1} \vert \vert \vert B \vert \vert \vert\right) \| A \| \end{array} \end{equation*}

The desired \(\tau \) can now be chosen to equal \(\max_{\| B \| = 1} \vert \vert \vert B \vert \vert \vert \text{.}\)
