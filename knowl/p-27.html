Pick \(\alpha \in \C \) with \(\vert \alpha \vert = 1 \) so that \(\alpha \widehat x^H \widehat y \) is real and nonnegative. Another way of saying this is that \(\alpha \widehat x^H \widehat y = \vert \widehat x^H \widehat y \vert \text{.}\) Note that since it is real we also know that \(\alpha \widehat x^H \widehat y = \overline{ \alpha \widehat x^H \widehat y } = \overline{\alpha} \widehat y^H \widehat x \text{.}\)
