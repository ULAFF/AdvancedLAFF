We show that the three conditions are met:

Let \(x, y \in \C^n \) and \(\alpha \in \C \) be arbitrarily chosen. Then

  • \(x \neq 0 \Rightarrow \| x \|_\infty > 0 \) (\(\| \cdot \|_\infty \) is positive definite):

    Notice that \(x \neq 0 \) means that at least one of its components is nonzero. Let's assume that \(\chi_j \neq 0 \text{.}\) Then

    \begin{equation*} \| x \|_\infty = \max_{i=0}^{n-1} \vert \chi_i \vert \ge \vert \chi_j \vert > 0. \end{equation*}

  • \(\| \alpha x \|_\infty = \vert \alpha \vert \| x \|_\infty \) (\(\| \cdot \|_\infty \) is homogeneous):

    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{lcl} \| \alpha x \|_\infty \amp =\amp \max_{i=0}^{n-1} \vert \alpha \chi_i \vert \\ \amp =\amp \max_{i=0}^{n-1} \vert \alpha \vert \vert \chi_i \vert \\ \amp =\amp \vert \alpha \vert \max_{i=0}^{n-1} \vert \chi_i \vert \\ \amp =\amp \vert \alpha \vert \| x \|_\infty. \end{array} \end{equation*}

  • \(\| x + y \|_\infty \leq \| x \|_\infty + \| y \|_\infty \) (\(\| \cdot \|_\infty \) obeys the triangle inequality):

    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{lcl} \| x + y \|_\infty \amp =\amp \max_{i=0}^{n-1} \vert \chi_i + \psi_i \vert \\ \amp \leq\amp \max_{i=0}^{n-1} ( \vert \chi_i \vert + \vert \psi_i \vert ) \\ \amp \leq\amp \max_{i=0}^{n-1} \vert \chi_i \vert + \max_{i=0}^{n-1} \vert \psi_i \vert \\ \amp = \amp \| x \|_\infty + \| y \|_\infty. \end{array} \end{equation*}
